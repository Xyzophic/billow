Billow reads your phone's motion sensors, which this device or browser doesn't expose.

Open this page on your iPhone (Safari) or Android phone (Chrome):
get started
  1. Tap enable motion sensors below and allow access.
  2. Place the phone flat on your belly so you can see the screen.
  3. Press reset.
  4. Take two breaths — Billow calibrates on them.
  5. Start a session at your desired length.
Tap the button above to begin. Your phone will ask permission to use its motion sensors.

Best position: lying flat on your back, phone just below the ribs. Billow auto-detects which way your breathing moves the phone, and the first reading takes about 30 seconds. Once it locks on, slow your breath — long, easy exhales — and watch the number drop. The screen stays awake during a session. All sensing happens on your phone — nothing is recorded or sent anywhere.
Billow is a relaxation and breathing-awareness tool, not a medical device. It does not diagnose, treat, or monitor any health condition. Readings are estimates and depend on phone placement. If you feel dizzy or short of breath, stop and breathe normally. Consult a clinician for any concerns about your breathing.
